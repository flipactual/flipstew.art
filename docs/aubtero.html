Aubtero This is a living document

  • Capitalists with private militaries hold most power
  • Deregulation, drugs, conflict between capitalists and socialists
  • Hope that somehow wealth will spread to the proletariat
  • Genocide and multiple generations of colonists each overthrowing the last
  • Cryptids, remote viewing division, last stand during genocide
  • Various cults vying for the favor of a single god
  • Money, land, power
  • Old name meaning “restful woods”
  • Once settled by Triokulans, making it very desirable, many groups have fought for this city and it has changed hands several times
  • Many businesses headquartered here
  • Mostly destitute in various forms living off mining town style rations
  • Bright colors adorn apartments throughout the city
  • People disappear - the government, capitalists, and unions are all suspected
  • Buildings are made of brick and stucco
  • A monument to Viktor Dellesentier, leader of the party who conquered Aubtero in the settle-up of 1532, stands in the middle of a paved courtyard originally used as a park in the middle of town
  • Most occupants of the city itself are employees of various companies producing literature, goods, materials, whatnot. Blue collar workers are typically unionized. White collar workers don’t make much more than their manual labor counterparts but a class war exists regardless. Most people simply strive to work less and fight less
  • Material goods largely leave the city by boat while research is communicated by radio and mail
  • Blues and theater and radio shows are immensely popular
  • Diverse food stalls exist in markets and large meals of rice and roots are served in homes
  • Alek Vitz returns to Aubtero from Witend to fulfill a book deal, lives above a restaurant, works with union leaders
  • Alek’s return sparks socialist unrest
  • Aubtero is coastal with fjords and megafauna and gardens and greenhouses
  • People move to the streets making their presence and the presence of their ideology known
  • The skys are free of pollutants
  • The Skeptic Sight is a local bookstore and meeting place for socialists often referred to as “The Site”
  • The church shifts to further favor the capitalists
  • Worldend begins to align politically with Witsend
  • The park has been replaced with a dog park (everything is going to shit)
  • People love to argue about Boont players, teams, and games
  • The nation is like the park, same as it always was, just with more beasts shitting in it
